## 0. The strategic thesis (one page)

**Cuisine Foods wins by owning a position no SA competitor holds: the single trusted partner for the oil going *into* a kitchen and the used oil coming *back out* — proven, local, and compliant, across Gauteng and the Western Cape.**

Three forces make this the right strategy, and all three came out of the research, not assumption:

1. **The market is fragmented and shallow.** Bulk-supply rivals are thin and weak on trust (one runs lorem-ipsum testimonials); the one deep UCO competitor (Coastal Oils) is Eastern-Cape-based and absent from our two target provinces. **Nobody owns Gauteng + Western Cape with deep content, real trust, and both sides of the oil lifecycle.** That gap is the whole opportunity.

2. **The closed loop is a moat, not a slogan.** Because the same restaurant both buys oil and discards it, the two offers share a customer, a sales conversation, and a delivery route. A UCO lead is a warm oil lead and vice-versa. Competitors who do one side can't credibly copy this without rebuilding their operation. It's also the internal-linking engine and the cross-sell mechanism — one idea doing SEO, conversion, and UX work at once.

3. **The demand is commercial and winnable.** UCO searches ("who buys used cooking oil", "used cooking oil collection Johannesburg", "sell used cooking oil") are high-intent and currently answered by Facebook groups, Gumtree, and thin sites. Supply searches are dominated by manufacturers with poor local SEO. Both are beatable with the OS's standard weapons: topical authority, real trust, genuine local pages, WhatsApp-first conversion.

**Everything below is the operational expression of that thesis.**

## 1. Website architecture — the two-pillar model

### The shape
```
                          ┌─────────── HOME (the fork) ───────────┐
                          │                                        │
              PILLAR A: BULK COOKING OIL SUPPLY      PILLAR B: USED COOKING OIL COLLECTION ★
              ├ Sunflower / Palm Olein / Soya        ├ How it works · Get paid (pricing)
              ├ Frying Oil (long-life)               ├ Compliance & safe-disposal certificate
              └ Buyer pages (restaurants, hotels,    ├ Grease-trap cleaning
                caterers, manufacturers, franchises) ├ UCO compliance reporting (franchises)
                          │                          └ Cooking oil recycling / biodiesel
                          │                                        │
                          └──── reciprocal internal links ─────────┘
                                          │
                     LOCATIONS: Gauteng ▸ (JHB · Pretoria · East Rand · Midrand-Centurion)
                                Western Cape ▸ (Cape Town · Northern Suburbs · Stellenbosch-Paarl)
                                          │
                     RESOURCES: [Frying & Oil cluster] + [UCO & Compliance cluster]
                                          │
                          About · Contact · Request a Quote · Thank-you
```
(Exact URLs, 34-page launch list and 301 map: `../PHASE-1-RESEARCH/09-ARCHITECTURE-…md`.)

### Why this architecture — three lenses
- **[SEO]** It's a textbook **hub-and-spoke topical map**. Two pillar hubs consolidate authority for the two head topics; product/service/buyer spokes capture mid-tail; location pages capture local intent; the content clusters capture informational intent and pass authority up to the money pages. Crossing *pillars × provinces* creates a defensible matrix (supply-Gauteng, UCO-Cape-Town, etc.) that a single-topic or single-province competitor can't match. Depth is ≤3 clicks from home — good for crawl and for link equity flow.
- **[CONV]** The architecture physically separates the **two opposite-polarity offers** (buying oil is a cost to minimise; UCO is cash + compliance relief). A visitor never has to untangle a blended message — they pick a lane at the hero and everything downstream (page, proof, CTA, pre-filled WhatsApp) matches that lane.
- **[UX]** Two big, obvious doors ("I need oil" / "I have used oil") map to how the customer already thinks about Cuisine Foods. Locations and Resources are secondary navigation for people who want to verify coverage or do research before enquiring. Nothing is more than a couple of decisions deep.

### The authority rule applied to pages
Every page answers **one** search intent and drives **one** primary conversion. A product page sells that product and asks for a quote; a location page proves local coverage and asks for a quote; an article answers a question and points to the money page. No page tries to do everything — that's what kills both ranking focus and conversion focus.

## 2. The user journey — four real buyers, mapped end to end

Personas come straight from the trigger-moment research (`../PHASE-1-RESEARCH/07`). For each: the trigger, the emotional state they arrive in, the path we design for them, and the conversion.

### Journey 1 — "Our supplier let us down / we ran out mid-service" (independent restaurant owner)
- **State:** distress, needs oil *today*. Arrives via `cooking oil supplier Johannesburg` or a Google Ad, often on mobile.
- **Path:** lands on Home or `/gauteng/johannesburg/` → hero instantly shows **Call + WhatsApp** in the same eyeline as the headline → taps WhatsApp (pre-filled "I need a bulk oil quote in Johannesburg") **or** click-to-call. Doesn't need to read the whole page.
- **Design consequence:** the distress buyer must be able to convert in **one tap, above the fold**, without a form. Reliability micro-proof ("delivering across Gauteng since 2009 *(Pending Confirmation)*") reassures in the same glance.
- **Conversion:** WhatsApp / Call. **[CONV/UX]**

### Journey 2 — "We're opening a second outlet / changing supplier on price" (procurement manager, small group)
- **State:** considered, high-value, documentation-driven. Researches quietly first (≈70% of the journey pre-contact), compares 2–3 suppliers.
- **Path:** Home → `/bulk-cooking-oil-supply/` → `/palm-olein/` (reads specs, heat-stability, 20L, delivery cadence) → checks `/about/` (years, branches, food-safety) → `/gauteng/` (coverage) → `/request-a-quote/` multi-step form (volume + sites qualifier).
- **Design consequence:** depth and proof matter more than speed. COA/quality language for the chef, reliability + terms for the owner, a form that captures monthly volume and number of sites so sales can prioritise.
- **Conversion:** qualified quote form. **[SEO seeds the research entry point; CONV closes with proof + qualifier.]**

### Journey 3 — "Inspection coming / drains blocking / need compliant disposal" (kitchen manager)
- **State:** trust-critical + urgent, UCO side. Searches `used cooking oil disposal` / `used cooking oil collection Cape Town`.
- **Path:** lands on `/used-cooking-oil-collection/` or `/western-cape/cape-town/` → immediately sees **licensed + safe-disposal certificate** promise → `/used-cooking-oil-collection/compliance/` confirms the by-law + certificate story → WhatsApp "arrange a compliant collection in Cape Town."
- **Design consequence:** compliance and certificate proof lead; the certificate is framed as *the buyer's* protection at inspection.
- **Conversion:** Arrange Collection (WhatsApp/form). **[CONV: answers the single highest-converting UCO objection first.]**

### Journey 4 — "We're throwing away oil we could sell" (owner / cost-conscious operator)
- **State:** opportunity-driven, UCO side. Searches `sell used cooking oil` / `who buys used cooking oil`.
- **Path:** lands on `/used-cooking-oil-collection/get-paid/` → sees "we pay per litre + free sealed drums + free collection" → WhatsApp "get paid for my used oil."
- **Design consequence:** the money hook leads, qualified by volume so we don't over-promise to a tiny generator.
- **Conversion:** Arrange Collection. **Cross-sell:** the thank-you page offers the *supply* side ("we also deliver your fresh oil"). This is the loop closing in real time. **[CONV]**

**The unifying design principle:** the homepage and location pages must simultaneously serve the one-tap distress buyer *and* the slow, proof-hungry procurement buyer. We do that by stacking **instant contact (WhatsApp/Call) + a trust bar** above the fold, then **depth + proof + form** below it. Fast buyers convert at the top; considered buyers scroll for evidence and convert at the bottom.

## 4. The homepage hero — the 5-second decision

The hero is the highest-leverage element on the site. It must pass the **5-second test**: a stranger should be able to say who this is, what they do, where, why to trust them, and what to do next — before they scroll.

### The 5 messages, mapped to on-screen elements
| Must communicate | Element |
|---|---|
| **Who** | Logo + eyebrow: *"Family-owned South African cooking-oil partner since 2009 *(Pending Confirmation)*"* |
| **What** | H1: the closed-loop supply + collection promise |
| **Where** | Sub-headline + trust bar: *"across Gauteng & the Western Cape"* |
| **Why trust** | Trust strip under the CTAs: years · 2 branches · licensed & compliant · thousands of kitchens *(Pending Confirmation)* |
| **Next action** | Two primary CTA buttons (the fork) + WhatsApp + Call in the same eyeline |

### Recommended hero copy (draft — final copy in BUILD)
> **Eyebrow:** Trusted by South African kitchens since 2009 *(Pending Client Confirmation)*
> **H1:** *Premium cooking oil in. Used cooking oil out. One trusted partner.*
> **Sub-head:** *Bulk sunflower, palm olein & soya delivered to restaurants, hotels and food manufacturers across **Gauteng & the Western Cape** — plus free, compliant collection of your used cooking oil.*
> **Primary CTAs:** `[ Get a Bulk Oil Quote ]`   `[ Arrange Used-Oil Collection ]`
> **Inline:** `WhatsApp us` · `Call 010 312 5275`
> **Trust strip:** 15+ years · Family-owned · Gauteng & Western Cape branches · Licensed UCO collector · Thousands of kitchens served *(items pending confirmation flagged in copy deck)*

### Why this hero — three lenses
- **[SEO]** The H1 carries the two head concepts (cooking oil supply + used cooking oil collection) and both provinces in natural language — a single H1 that targets the site's two primary topics and its geography without keyword-stuffing. It reinforces the exact entities the rest of the site is built around.
- **[CONV]** The **two-button fork** is the most important conversion decision on the page: it routes the two journeys instantly and pre-qualifies the lead (the button they press pre-fills the WhatsApp/quote intent). WhatsApp + Call sit in the same eyeline so the distress buyer converts without scrolling. The trust strip answers "why trust you" in the same glance that asks for the click.
- **[UX]** One clear headline, one clear choice of two doors, contact always visible. No carousel, no competing messages, no cognitive load. The closed-loop headline is memorable and differentiating — it says in one line what no competitor can claim.

### Hero mechanics
- **LCP discipline:** the hero image is the LCP element — priority-loaded, AVIF/WebP <200KB, explicit dimensions (no CLS). Headline renders in system/`next/font` immediately (`display:swap`).
- **Placeholder now:** hero uses the config-driven `<Placeholder>` (aspect-ratio box, branded) until the client's photography lands — swap with no code change. Recommended shot: a branded delivery vehicle or drums at a real kitchen (proves both sides of the loop).
- **Reduced motion:** any hero animation respects `prefers-reduced-motion`; motion is a subtle fade/rise, never a blocking animation.

## 5. Service & product page strategy

Three page *types* on the supply side, three on the UCO side — each a reusable template (detailed section-by-section in `01-PAGE-TEMPLATES.md`).

### 5a. Pillar hubs — `/bulk-cooking-oil-supply/` and `/used-cooking-oil-collection/`
- **[SEO]** Target the head terms ("bulk cooking oil supplier", "used cooking oil collection"); consolidate authority; link down to every spoke and out to location pages. These are the pages we most want to rank and to earn links to.
- **[CONV]** Present the full offer + the dominant 2–3 objections for that side, then route to the specific spoke or straight to a quote.
- **[UX]** The "menu" for that side of the business — a visitor who isn't sure which product/service they need starts here.

### 5b. Product money pages — `/sunflower-oil/`, `/palm-olein/`, `/soya-oil/`, `/frying-oil/`
- **[SEO]** One product = one page = one primary keyword cluster (e.g. *palm olein supplier / 20L palm oil price / heat-stable frying oil*). Product schema (offers = quote, no fake price/review). Soya is a **competitive gap** — fewer strong SA players — so it's a priority ranking target, not an afterthought.
- **[CONV]** Speak to *both* deciders: the chef (specs, smoke point, fry-life, consistency/COA) and the owner (bulk pricing, reliable delivery, 20L "no strict minimum"). Inline quote CTA + WhatsApp on every product page (fixing the current site's friction where every CTA bounces to /contact).
- **[UX]** Scannable: what it is → who it's for → why it performs → how it's delivered → quote. A buyer can compare products in seconds.

### 5c. Buyer-segment pages — `/cooking-oil-for-restaurants/`, `…-hotels/`, `…-caterers/`, `…-food-manufacturers/`, `…-franchises/`
- **[SEO]** Capture segment-qualified intent ("cooking oil for restaurants", "bulk vegetable oil manufacturer") and build breadth around the supply pillar.
- **[CONV]** Message-match to the segment's specific needs (manufacturers want COA + tanker/IBC + traceability; franchises want multi-site rollout + audit-ready certs; restaurants want price + reliability). These double as **Google Ads landing pages** (see §12).
- **[UX]** "This supplier understands *my* kind of kitchen" — self-identification lowers friction and raises perceived fit.

### 5d. UCO service pages — `get-paid/`, `compliance/`, `grease-trap-cleaning/`, `uco-compliance-reporting/`, `cooking-oil-recycling/`
Each isolates one UCO intent + objection (pricing transparency; compliance/certificate; grease-trap; franchise reporting; sustainability). Rationale detailed in `../PHASE-1-RESEARCH/04-UCO-STRATEGY.md` §6. **[SEO]** each captures a distinct query cluster no competitor fully owns; **[CONV]** each answers one objection and asks for a collection booking; **[UX]** a UCO visitor lands exactly where their specific worry is addressed.

## 6. Internal linking strategy — the semantic engine

Internal links are how we turn a collection of pages into a topical authority and how we shepherd visitors toward a quote. Five rules (full detail in `../PHASE-1-RESEARCH/09` §3):

1. **Hub-and-spoke** — pillars link down to all spokes; spokes link up + to 2–3 siblings. Descriptive anchors ("bulk **palm olein** supply"), never "click here". **[SEO]** distributes authority and defines topical relationships; **[UX]** natural next steps.
2. **The closed loop (signature move)** — every supply page links to UCO and every UCO page links to supply. **[SEO]** binds the two pillars into one entity graph; **[CONV]** the cross-sell; **[UX]** completes the mental model.
3. **Location interlink** — province ⇄ metro children; metros link to relevant supply + UCO pillars; pillars link out to both provinces. **[SEO]** fuses topical + local relevance (the supply-Gauteng, UCO-Cape-Town matrix).
4. **Content → money** — every article links to the money page it supports; money pages cite 1–2 supporting articles for depth. **[SEO]** passes informational authority to commercial pages + demonstrates E-E-A-T; **[CONV]** moves researchers toward enquiry.
5. **Global + breadcrumbs** — header exposes both pillars, Locations, Resources; footer carries the full location list + key money pages + both branch NAPs; breadcrumbs (with schema) everywhere. **[SEO]** no orphan pages, clear hierarchy; **[UX]** always-available wayfinding.

**Semantic relevance:** each cluster deliberately uses its entity set (§7 of `../PHASE-1-RESEARCH/05`) so Google reads the site as *about* cooking-oil supply and UCO in Gauteng/WC — not a thin brochure. Anchor-text variety is planned, not repeated exact-match.

## 7. Content cluster strategy — topical authority that sells

Two clusters, each a pillar + supporting articles, engineered to build authority **and** feed the money pages (not to chase traffic for its own sake).

### Cluster 1 — "Frying & Cooking Oil" → supports the **Supply** pillar
Articles: best oil for commercial deep frying in SA · what is palm olein · sunflower vs palm vs soya for frying · smoke points reference · when to change fryer oil / how long it lasts · how to cut frying-oil cost per litre · the commercial frying best-practice guide (expand the existing one).
- **[SEO]** owns the informational layer above the product pages; each article targets a real question and links down to the relevant product; establishes Cuisine Foods as an oil *expert*, not just a seller (E-E-A-T). US sites currently own "best oil for deep frying" — an SA-localised gap.
- **[CONV]** captures top-of-funnel researchers (chefs, kitchen managers) and routes them to product money pages.
- **[UX]** genuinely useful — the kind of content a chef bookmarks, which builds brand affinity before the buying trigger even fires.

### Cluster 2 — "Used Cooking Oil & Compliance" ★ → supports the **UCO** pillar
Articles: how to dispose of UCO legally in SA · is it legal to reuse cooking oil in a restaurant (R1316 / Foodstuffs Act / grease-trap regs) · UCO price per litre — what to expect in 2026 · how much UCO does a restaurant produce · how UCO becomes biodiesel · grease traps & FOG by-laws: what an inspector checks · why to only use a licensed collector.
- **[SEO]** builds the compliance/pricing authority **no Gauteng/WC competitor owns**; strong FAQ/AI-Overview territory; the pricing article doubles as a money query.
- **[CONV]** every compliance worry answered here ends with "arrange a compliant collection"; the pricing article converts the "sell my oil" searcher.
- **[UX]** positions Cuisine Foods as the trustworthy, knowledgeable, *legitimate* operator in a market full of Gumtree cash buyers — which is itself the differentiator.

**Publishing discipline (OS):** each article = one intent, an FAQ block (FAQPage schema), an author/E-E-A-T signal, internal links up to pillar + across to a money page, one CTA. Quality over volume — a handful of authoritative pieces beats twenty thin posts.

## 8. Conversion strategy (summary — full detail in `../PHASE-1-RESEARCH/07`)

- **Two opposite-polarity offers, one buyer:** fork in the hero, separate service pages, cross-sell only on the thank-you step.
- **WhatsApp-first** (SA reality: ≈94% penetration, reply 8–10× email) with **page-specific pre-filled messages**; **click-to-call** for distress; **multi-step qualifying quote form** for procurement buyers (volume + #sites fields quietly qualify and filter one-offs).
- **Every section answers Q1/Q2/Q3** (trust / contact / choose-us). Objection-mapped section order (§3).
- **Trust bar under the hero** so every trigger-state sees credibility first.
- **CTA wording matched to trigger:** "Get a Bulk Oil Quote", "Get Paid for Your Used Oil", "Arrange Free Collection", "Call Now" — never "Submit"/"Contact Us".
- **Lead quality by design:** quote-only pricing + "for business/trade" framing + volume qualifier attract recurring accounts and screen out tyre-kickers.

**The measurable goal:** every page has one primary conversion; every conversion path is tracked (form → GHL webhook → thank-you; `tel:`; WhatsApp) so we optimise on *qualified enquiries*, not traffic.

## 9. Local SEO strategy — dominating two provinces over time

Beyond location pages: a **compounding local-authority program** (full tactics in `../PHASE-1-RESEARCH/06`).

### How we dominate Gauteng and Western Cape
- **Two Google Business Profiles** (one per province, real staffed premises, address hidden, service areas defined) — the anchors for two independent map-pack presences.
- **Province pillar → metro child pages**, each with genuinely unique local content (named suburbs/industrial nodes, local routes/lead times, local customer clusters, area FAQ, area testimonial). The anti-doorway rule is the guardrail — competitor city pages are thin, which is precisely our opening.
- **Full SA citation stack** with byte-consistent +27 NAP (GBP, Bing, Apple, HelloPeter, saYellow under "Oils – Waste & Collectors", Brabys, Cylex, Snupit, Nichemarket) + the **ROSE Foundation collectors listing** (high-trust UCO citation).
- **Per-province LocalBusiness + Service schema** with `areaServed` (never fabricated per-city addresses).

### Building local authority *over time* (the flywheel)
1. **Reviews as a velocity engine** — driver + WhatsApp review-request flow after every smooth delivery/collection; ask reviewers to name their area. B2B has few clients, so velocity and area-mentions matter more than volume.
2. **Local content cadence** — GBP posts rotating area names ("now collecting across the East Rand"); location-specific article angles as data justifies.
3. **Local links** — restaurant/hospitality associations, approved-supplier listings on client sites, chambers of commerce, biodiesel-partner cross-links, local PR (SA loves used-oil "eco" stories).
4. **Phase-2 metro expansion** — promote Sandton, Soweto, West Rand / Southern Suburbs, Somerset West, Atlantic Seaboard to their own pages *only once* the tier-1 metros are indexed and pulling impressions (data-driven, not speculative).

**Why over time:** local authority is cumulative — reviews, citations, links and area-tagged content compound. The competitor field is static and thin, so consistent monthly motion pulls Cuisine Foods clear within both provinces.

## 12. Google Ads readiness (built in from day one)

The architecture is **PPC-ready by design** — the same pages that rank also convert paid traffic, and several are purpose-built to become dedicated landing pages.

- **Message-match landing pages already exist in the map:** product pages (`/palm-olein/`, `/frying-oil/`), buyer-segment pages (`/cooking-oil-for-restaurants/`, `…-franchises/`), UCO conversion pages (`/used-cooking-oil-collection/get-paid/`, `/grease-trap-cleaning/`), and location pages. Each has a single intent, a single primary CTA, trust signals, and minimal friction — the exact anatomy of a good paid landing page.
- **Campaign-to-page mapping:**
  - *Search — Supply:* "bulk cooking oil supplier [city]" → location or product page.
  - *Search — UCO cash:* "sell used cooking oil [city]" → `get-paid/`.
  - *Search — UCO compliance:* "used cooking oil disposal / collection [city]" → `compliance/` or location page.
  - *Search — segment:* "cooking oil for restaurants" → buyer-segment page.
- **Reusable sections for paid:** hero, trust bar, objection blocks, FAQ and CTA band are componentised, so a stripped **dedicated PPC variant** (no nav, one CTA, one message) can be assembled from existing components without new design — an OS pattern.
- **Conversion tracking parity:** the same GA4/GTM events (form, call, WhatsApp) that measure organic measure paid, so ROAS is visible per campaign.
- **Architecture supports expansion:** new campaigns get a message-matched page under the existing pillar/location structure — no re-architecture, no orphan landing pages.
- **Future:** dedicated `/lp/` variants can be added later without touching the SEO site; the component library makes them cheap.
