## The one-paragraph story

Cuisine Foods is a 15-year-old (est. 2009), family-owned South African business that **both supplies** premium bulk cooking oil (Sunflower, Palm Olein, Soya) to professional kitchens **and collects** their used cooking oil for recycling into biodiesel. It claims to be *"one of the two biggest players in South Africa's used cooking oil industry."* It has **two real physical branches — Centurion (Gauteng) and Montague Gardens, Cape Town (Western Cape)** — sitting directly in the two provinces we're told to dominate. Yet the current website mentions neither city in any indexable heading, has zero location pages, no trust signals, thin content, broken/missing technical SEO, and buries its single most ownable asset: the **closed-loop "we supply your oil AND collect it back" model** that almost no competitor offers. **The opportunity is not to invent a story — it's to properly build the one that already exists.**

---

## The five decisions that will define this build

### 1. Lead with the closed loop — "One partner for the oil going in and the oil coming out"
This is the most ownable position in the market. Research confirmed that ranking competitors almost all specialise on **one** side: either bulk supply (Golden Fry, Nature's Best) or UCO collection (Coastal Oils, AJ Oils, Rein Oil). Cuisine Foods credibly does both, in both target provinces. Every page should reinforce this loop and internally link supply ↔ UCO. A UCO lead is a warm oil lead, and vice versa.

### 2. Give UCO the weight it deserves — as a co-equal pillar, not a footnote
Per the brief, UCO is a top SEO priority *without overshadowing the core business*. The architecture treats **Bulk Cooking Oil Supply** and **Used Cooking Oil Collection** as **two co-equal pillars**, each with product/service pages, location pages, and a content cluster. UCO is genuinely winnable: SERPs are full of thin, dated, single-certificate sites and Facebook/Gumtree listings, and no single competitor owns Gauteng + Western Cape with deep content + real trust. (See `04-UCO-STRATEGY.md`.)

### 3. Own local — the single biggest, easiest SEO win
Almost no competitor targets cities (only Rein Oil has thin JHB/Cape Town pages). Cuisine Foods has two real branches to anchor legitimate local SEO. Plan: **two province pillar pages (Gauteng, Western Cape) + metro child pages (Johannesburg, Pretoria, East Rand, Midrand-Centurion / Cape Town, Northern Suburbs, Stellenbosch-Paarl)**, two Google Business Profiles, and a full SA citation stack — each page carrying genuinely unique local content (anti-doorway). (See `06-LOCAL-SEO-STRATEGY.md`.)

### 4. Build for conversion around WhatsApp + a two-path fork
In SA, WhatsApp is the dominant B2B channel (≈94% of internet users; reply rates ~8–10× email). Primary CTA = WhatsApp with page-specific pre-filled messages; secondary = click-to-call (for "we ran out mid-service" distress buyers); tertiary = a multi-step qualifying quote form (for procurement-driven hotels/franchises/manufacturers). The hero forks the two journeys — **"Order Bulk Oil"** vs **"Arrange Used-Oil Collection"** — and cross-sells only on the thank-you step. (See `07-CONVERSION-STRATEGY.md`.)

### 5. Fix the trust vacuum — it's how we beat everyone
Every competitor is weak on trust (Golden Fry literally uses lorem-ipsum "J.K-Rowling" testimonials; Coastal rests on one certificate; Oil Man is "awaiting ISO"). Cuisine Foods can win outright by stacking **real** proof: 15 years, family-owned, "two biggest UCO players," licensed/compliant status + the **safe-disposal certificate**, food-safety/quality handling, named client *types*, delivery-fleet proof, and a real Google review flow. Nothing fabricated — only what's `[verified]` in the Client Brief may appear as copy.

## What's broken on the current site (redesign must fix)
- **No location targeting** — "South Africa/nationwide" only; cities appear solely in the contact dropdown. Cape Town branch missing from schema entirely.
- **Thin content** — short benefit-bullet pages; only one resource article ("Guide to Frying"). Low topical authority.
- **Technical SEO holes** — empty/missing robots.txt, www vs non-www inconsistency, schema typo ("Barlolong"), no canonical on home, only LocalBusiness schema (no Product/Service/FAQ/Org/Breadcrumb), orphaned 404s (`/used-cooking-oil-uco/`).
- **Trust vacuum** — no testimonials, no certs shown, no B-BBEE, inconsistent founding date ("2009" vs "over a decade" vs "15+ years").
- **Conversion friction** — no inline quote forms on product pages; the high-value `/uco-report` lead magnet has *no form*.

## The prize (why this is winnable)
- **Bulk supply SERPs:** fragmented, thin, weak trust, no location pages → winnable on SEO/trust/UX basics.
- **UCO SERPs:** one real benchmark (Coastal Oils, ISCC — but Eastern-Cape based, thin geography/social proof) → beatable by owning Gauteng + Western Cape with deeper content + real trust.
- SA UCO market ≈ US$277M (2024) → ~US$550M (2035); collectors **pay restaurants R4–R8/litre** — a powerful, under-exploited "get paid for your waste oil" hook.
