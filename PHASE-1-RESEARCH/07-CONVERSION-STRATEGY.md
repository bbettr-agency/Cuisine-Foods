# CONVERSION STRATEGY — B2B lead generation (not ecommerce)

Every page exists to raise the probability of a *qualified* enquiry via four conversions: **Request a Quote · Call · Arrange UCO Collection · Become a recurring oil customer.** `[E]` evidence-backed · `[R]` reasoned.

## The structural insight
Cuisine Foods runs **two opposite-polarity offers to overlapping buyers.** Buying oil is a *cost* the buyer wants to minimise + de-risk; UCO collection is *cash + compliance relief* they want to maximise + simplify. The same restaurant is often both. The site must serve both journeys cleanly, never blend the two value props in one block, and cross-sell only at the thank-you step — that two-sided loop is the biggest lever. `[R]`

## 1. Buying psychology
- **Bulk oil = modified rebuy, not new purchase.** You're the *replacement* supplier — sell **switching confidence**, not novelty. In edible-oil procurement, stability/consistency/documentation outrank lowest price. `[E]`
- **Decision-makers vary:** independent = owner (fast, WhatsApp, price+reliability); hotel/caterer/manufacturer = procurement (spec, COA, SLA, terms); chef specs quality, owner signs cost; franchise = HQ specifies centrally (target the group, not the outlet). `[E/R]`
- **Cycle:** independent single-site = days (often distress-driven); multi-site/manufacturer/franchise = weeks–months, research quietly then enquire warm (~70% of journey pre-contact). `[E]`
- **Prize = the recurring account** (50–200× a one-off). Engineer the site to attract + signal fit for recurring relationships. `[R]`
- **UCO = low-consideration, trust/compliance purchase** — a problem to make *gone, legal, ideally profitable*; shorter cycle, lower price-sensitivity, higher trust-sensitivity. `[E/R]`

## 2. Triggers → arrival state → site consequence
| Trigger | Side | State | Consequence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Supplier let us down / ran out mid-service | Oil | Distress | Instant Call + WhatsApp above fold; lead with reliability/fast delivery |
| Price hiked again | Oil | Price-driven | Reframe to total cost + reliability; "get a competitive quote" |
| Opening a new outlet / scaling | Oil | Considered, high-value | Signal capacity, multi-site delivery, account mgmt; qualifying form |
| Oil breaks down too fast | Oil | Quality-seeking | Fry-life/performance/consistency; chef-facing COA proof |
| Drains blocking / grease trap overflow | UCO | Distress | Collection = the fix; fast WhatsApp booking |
| Inspection due / need certificate | UCO | Trust-critical, urgent | Lead with **safe-disposal certificate** + licensed status (highest-converting UCO msg) |
| Want to get paid for waste oil | UCO | Opportunity | "We pay for your used oil" + free drums + free collection |
| ESG / recycling pressure | UCO | Considered | Sustainability/biodiesel + certificate proof |

**Design implication:** hero serves distress (instant call/WhatsApp) *and* considered (quote form + proof) simultaneously; never force a distress buyer through a long form. 5-min response is the B2B benchmark; in SA that speed lives on WhatsApp. `[E]`

## 4. Trust signals — ranked for this niche
1. Reliable-supply proof (fleet, schedule promise, kitchens served, years) — #1 procurement differentiator. `[E]`
2. Compliance + safe-disposal certificate (UCO). `[E]`
3. Food-safety/quality proof — COA, HACCP-aligned handling, specs. `[E]`
4. Real client types/logos (even category: "national franchise groups"). `[R]`
5. Scale/market position ("two biggest UCO players", certified facility). `[R]`
6. WhatsApp responsiveness (a live fast number *is* trust). `[E]`
7. Years + physical address + company registration (anti-fly-by-night). `[R]`
8. Reviews/testimonials — a named "never missed a delivery in 3 years" quote beats star ratings. `[R]`

**Placement:** trust bar directly under the hero, visible to every trigger-state before they scroll.

## 5. Conversion mechanics (SA)
- **Primary = WhatsApp** (floating + inline), page-specific pre-filled message ("…quote on bulk cooking oil" vs "…arrange used-oil collection"). SA: ≈94% on WhatsApp, ~80%+ open, reply 8–10× email. `[E]`
- **Secondary = Click-to-call** (header on mobile) for distress triggers.
- **Tertiary = multi-step qualifying quote form** for documentation-driven procurement (forms still highest-converting for ~50% of B2B). `[E]`
- **Form:** Step 1 low-friction — *I want to →* [Buy oil]/[Arrange collection] · name · WhatsApp · business name. Step 2 qualifier — business type · **monthly oil volume** (oil) or **litres/week used oil** (UCO) · # sites · suburb. Those two qualifier fields surface high-value recurring buyers + quietly deprioritise one-offs (no scary "minimum order" wall). `[E/R]`
- **Two journeys:** hero forks "Order Bulk Cooking Oil" vs "Arrange Used-Oil Collection"; each inherits its own service page + pre-filled CTA; **cross-sell on the thank-you step only.** `[R]`
- **Quote UX:** "Request a Quote" → dedicated quote page (not generic Contact); confirm "we'll WhatsApp your quote within X business hours."

## 6. Section order
**Home:** (1) Hero + two-path fork + WhatsApp/phone in eyeline → (2) Trust bar → (3) two side-by-side offer cards → (4) Why Cuisine / reliability proof → (5) UCO compliance + certificate → (6) social proof → (7) how it works (both) → (8) FAQ → (9) final CTA band (WhatsApp+Call+Quote + response-time promise).
**Service page:** focused hero → the 2–3 dominant objections for that side → proof (COA/fleet for oil; certificate/licensed for UCO) → how it works + who it's for → trust → side-specific FAQ → quote CTA + cross-sell teaser.

## 7. CTA wording
Oil: "Get a Bulk Oil Quote", "Request Today's Oil Price". UCO: "Arrange Free Collection", "Get Paid for Your Used Oil", "Book a Compliant Collection". Distress: "Call Now" / "WhatsApp Us Now". **Never** "Submit"/"Contact Us"; always pair button with a benefit or response-time microcopy.

## 8. Lead qualification
Attract recurring: copy uses "reliable weekly delivery / dedicated account manager / multi-site supply / trade & account terms"; franchise/group lane targets HQ. Filter one-offs: no published list prices (quote-only protects margin + deters price-shoppers), sensible minimum in FAQ, "for business/trade" framing, a "household/personal" form option routed to low-priority.
