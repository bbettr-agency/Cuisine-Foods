## 1. Why UCO is the right bet
- **The market is real and growing.** SA UCO market ≈ **US$277M (2024) → ~US$550M (2035)**, driven by biodiesel demand and energy costs. `[verified — Market Research Future]`
- **Search demand is active and commercial.** SERPs for "who buys used cooking oil", "used cooking oil collection [city]", "sell used cooking oil", "used cooking oil price per litre" are full of dedicated competitors *and* Facebook groups / Gumtree listings — i.e. real high-intent B2B demand that thin players currently soak up. `[verified — SERP]`
- **It's winnable.** Only **Coastal Oils** has deep, modern, certificate-backed content — and it's Eastern-Cape based, thin on Gauteng/WC geography and social proof. Everyone else (AJ Oils, Rein Oil, Oil Man, OilDrop) is dated, blog-less, price-opaque. **No single competitor owns Gauteng + Western Cape with deep content + real trust.** `[verified]`
- **It compounds the core business.** UCO and supply share the *same customer* (a restaurant that buys oil also discards it). The closed loop is the differentiator and the internal-link engine.

## 2. How the SA UCO model actually works (so the copy is correct)
- **Value chain:** Generators (kitchens) → Collectors (scheduled/on-demand pickup, own fleet, free drums) → Processors (filter/test/store) → End-users (**biodiesel/UCOME** primarily; oleochemicals; export as ISCC-certified feedstock). `[verified]`
- **Collectors PAY generators.** UCO has resale value, so removal is not charged — the dominant model is **cash for oil ≈ R4–R8/litre** (Bargain Oil publishes **R5–R7/L**; ~R4/L quoted in Gauteng; some to R8/L), varying by region, volume and quality; a minority collect free (e.g. Scope Oils). Payment via COD or weigh-then-pay (VGM). `[verified]`
- **No minimums, drums provided.** Most advertise "any quantity" and supply/loan sealed containers (also prevents illegal reuse). `[verified]`
- **Legitimate collectors refuse the food chain.** Reputable operators explicitly do **not** route UCO to animal feed or human consumption — a trust point to state plainly. `[verified — e.g. Oil Man KZN]`

## 3. The compliance angle = the highest-converting UCO message
This is the buyer's strongest non-price motivation and where thin competitors are weakest.
- **The regime (get the distinction right):** SA's hazardous "used oil" system (SAWIS, ROSE Foundation, HW07, safe-disposal-certificate-as-manifest) is built primarily for **petroleum/lubricant oil**. Restaurant **vegetable UCO/FOG** is governed by **municipal wastewater by-laws + food-safety law (Foodstuffs Act; R1316 Edible Fats & Oils regs) + NEMWA duty-of-care**. Don't blur the two. `[verified]`
- **Municipal FOG by-laws (Cape Town, Johannesburg, eThekwini):** fats/oils/grease down the drain is **illegal**; **grease traps are mandatory** for food premises (clean ≈ every 30 days); illegal dumping → substantial fines / possible closure; the **waste transfer / disposal certificate must be kept ≥2 years** for inspection. `[verified]`
- **Duty of care:** the generator remains liable for their waste — using a **licensed collector who issues a certificate** is how they discharge that liability at an inspection. `[verified]`
- **Food-safety hook (use carefully, factually):** repeatedly reused/degraded oil forms toxic compounds (acrylamide — WHO carcinogen; PAHs; aldehydes). Illegal re-selling of UCO back into food is a documented food-fraud risk (a 1997 Pretoria study found high-polar, reused oil in circulation) — the reason to insist on a **certified, licensed** collector. `[verified — general/historic; no recent named SA raid found — do not imply one]`

**Copy consequence:** the UCO pillar leads with three promises — **(1) we pay you for your oil, (2) free sealed drums + collection on your schedule, (3) a compliance/safe-disposal certificate that protects you at inspection** — backed by licensed/certified status. Verify Cuisine's exact credentials (Client Brief §11 Q3–4) before publishing.

- Home hero forks **two equal paths** (Order Oil / Arrange Collection) — neither dominates.
- Supply keeps its own pillar, product pages, buyer pages and content cluster (`05` + `09`).
