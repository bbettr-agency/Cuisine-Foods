# Conversion-First Adversarial Review
### Judged through three equal lenses — Conversion · User Experience · SEO/AI — grounded in the live pages

**Standard we're holding to:** the highest-converting industrial B2B website in South Africa that also becomes the authoritative source for Google + AI search. Conversion first; SEO/GEO in service of it. **No section survives unless it improves BOTH conversion and SEO.**

The six questions, applied honestly to the current commercial pages (home, product, UCO, location, quote):
*Is every section earning its place? · Can it convert better? · Is the next action obvious? · Are we removing friction? · Would it convince a procurement manager in <30s? · Does it feel like a premium national supplier?*

---

## What's genuinely working (keep)
- **Two-path hero fork** (gold "Get a Bulk Oil Quote" + black "Arrange Used-Oil Collection") + WhatsApp/Call in one eyeline — clear next action, serves both journeys.
- **WhatsApp-first stack** (floating + sticky mobile bar) — right for SA B2B.
- **UCO value calculator** — a genuine conversion differentiator no competitor has.
- **Premium black/gold/cream theme**, real branded photography in the hero, honest quote-based data.
- **Closed-loop differentiator** ("oil in / oil out") stated in the hero.

## What's failing the standard (fix — findings are specific, from the live DOM)

### 1. Redundant heading hierarchy on product pages — hurts UX, SEO *and* scannability
On `/palm-olein` the H1 is "Palm Olein" and the very next H2 is **also "Palm Olein"** (the intro section repeats the title). It reads as a stutter, wastes the most valuable heading, and adds a scroll before any value lands. **Fix:** replace the repeated title with a benefit-led heading (or drop the heading and lead straight with the value + keypoints).

### 2. CTA cadence is too sparse — friction for a convinced buyer
Product/UCO pages carry a strong CTA at the **top** (hero) and **bottom** (CTA band) — but **nothing in between**, across specs + two content sections + two link-lists + FAQ. A procurement manager convinced by the spec table has to scroll to the bottom or back to the top to act. **Fix:** a mid-page "request a quote / WhatsApp" strip after the value/spec sections. (Every commercial page should never be more than ~1.5 screens from a CTA.)

### 3. Trust is under-proven on the commercial pages — the #1 conversion gap
The homepage has a thin trust bar (since 2009 · 2 branches · family-owned · licensed). **The product, UCO and location pages have NO trust/proof/social-proof block at all.** A procurement manager on `/palm-olein` sees specs but no reliability proof, no "why us over another supplier", no client validation. This is exactly what fails the <30-second procurement test. **Fix:** a compact, reusable **"Why Cuisine Foods / proof"** strip on every commercial page — closed-loop differentiator + reliability proof + (progressive) certifications, volumes, client types, guarantee, response time. It's conversion *and* E-E-A-T.

### 4. Premium photography is under-used — the site doesn't yet *feel* like a national supplier on inner pages
We have ~97 real shoot photos; only ~11 are placed, almost all on the homepage/about. Product and UCO pages are **text + tables only** below the hero. That reads "small supplier," not "premium national operation," and it hurts scannability. **Fix:** place operational/product photography mid-page on commercial templates (fleet/warehouse/collection/product) — premium feel + trust + a visual break, with descriptive alt (SEO).

### 5. Stacked SEO link-lists dilute conversion focus
Product pages render **"Related pages" AND "Related guides"** as two separate link-list sections low on the page, pushing the CTA down and adding clutter. They help SEO but as two blocks they cost conversion focus. **Fix:** consolidate into ONE tight "Related" block, and place the mid-page CTA *before* it so the commercial action comes first.

### 6. Objection handling is reactive (buried in FAQ), not proactive
The top procurement objections — *will you deliver on time? what's the minimum? do you cover my area? how fast can we switch? is the price competitive?* — are only answered if the visitor opens the FAQ. **Fix:** a visible **"What to expect / why switching is easy"** reassurance block on commercial pages (on-time delivery, no strict minimum, both provinces, first delivery this week, no lock-in). These are answer-first passages → also FAQ/AI-Overview fuel.

### 7. Homepage proves less than it should
Strong structure, but beyond the trust bar there's no hard proof (a real testimonial, client logos, a scale number). The hero H1 is memorable but abstract for a scanning procurement buyer — the subhead rescues it. **Fix:** a testimonial/proof slot (renders when the client provides real quotes/logos — progressive, no fabrication) and a slightly harder proof point in the trust bar when data lands.

## The plan — "Conversion Excellence" phase (every item improves BOTH conversion and SEO)

Ordered by conversion impact. Nothing here is "content for SEO's sake"; each earns both.

| # | Change | Conversion | UX | SEO/AI |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Reusable "Why Cuisine Foods" proof strip** on all commercial pages (closed-loop + reliability + progressive certs/volumes/clients/guarantee/response) | Trust, differentiation, 30-sec test | Consistent reassurance | E-E-A-T, entity, answer content |
| 2 | **Mid-page CTA strip** on money pages (quote + WhatsApp) | Removes friction, catches convinced buyers | Action always near | engagement signals |
| 3 | **Proactive objection/"what to expect" block** (on-time, no minimum, both provinces, switch this week, no lock-in) | Kills top objections early | Scannable reassurance | answer-first → FAQ/AIO |
| 4 | **Fix redundant H2** + tighten intro to lead with value | Faster value | Clean hierarchy | correct H-structure |
| 5 | **Deploy premium photography** mid-page on product/UCO/why-us (fleet, warehouse, collection, product) | Premium-supplier feel, trust | Visual break, scannability | image alt/context |
| 6 | **Consolidate "Related pages/guides"** into one tight block, CTA before it | Commercial action first | Less clutter | keeps internal links |
| 7 | **Homepage proof/testimonial slot** (progressive) + harder trust-bar proof | Social proof | — | E-E-A-T |

**Guardrail applied:** if a change only helped SEO (e.g., another article), it's **not** in this plan. Commercial pages first. Growth (more location/content pages) stays paused until the commercial pages hit this standard.

**Client data that makes trust land hardest (progressive — site works without it, gets stronger with it):** 2–3 real testimonials (+consent), client names/types or logos, confirmed certifications (ISCC/HACCP/B-BBEE), a scale number (e.g. litres delivered/month), a service guarantee, and the real WhatsApp response time.
